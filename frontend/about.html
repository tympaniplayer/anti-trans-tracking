About This Project

The Anti-Trans Legislation Tracker is an automated, open-data tool for monitoring legislation targeting transgender people across all levels of United States government — federal, state, and local.

How It Works

Every day, our automated scraper searches the LegiScan legislative database for bills related to transgender rights using a comprehensive set of keywords. Each bill is automatically categorized by topic and its status is tracked over time.

Categories

CategoryDescription
HealthcareBills restricting gender-affirming care (puberty blockers, hormones, surgery)
SportsBills banning transgender athletes from school/college sports
BathroomsBills restricting access to bathrooms and changing facilities
EducationBills restricting discussion of gender identity in schools, pronoun policies
DragBills restricting drag performances or shows
Identity DocumentsBills restricting changes to gender markers on IDs and birth certificates
Religious ExemptionsBills providing religious exemptions to refuse services to trans people
DefinitionsBills defining sex in law as biological sex at birth

Data Sources

All legislative data is sourced from LegiScan, which provides comprehensive legislative tracking for all 50 states, the District of Columbia, and the United States Congress.

Open Data

All tracked bill data is available as open JSON files that anyone can use:

  • bills.json — All tracked bills with summary data
  • states.json — Per-state summary and counts
  • metadata.json — Overall statistics and last update time

Limitations

  • Bill categorization is automated and may occasionally miscategorize bills
  • Local/municipal legislation coverage depends on LegiScan's data availability
  • The tracker identifies bills by keyword matching — some relevant bills may be missed if they use unusual language
  • Data updates daily; there may be a delay between a bill's introduction and its appearance here
